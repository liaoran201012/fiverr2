The Buyer’s Checklist

Use this before you place an order to reduce surprises and extra costs.

Step 1: Shortlist 5–10 relevant sellers and compare scope, delivery time, and recent reviews.
Step 2: Read the gig details carefully—deliverables, number of revisions, and what’s excluded.
Step 3: Ask for recent work samples that match your use case and industry.
Step 4: Scan 1–3 star reviews to understand common issues and how the seller responded.
Step 5: Message before ordering. Align on scope, timeline, file formats, and handoff expectations.
Step 6: Review deliveries promptly so any fixes can be handled within the revision window.

Practical Ways to Save

Responsible, policy-friendly tips to help your budget go further.

Compare Quotes: Ask two or three shortlisted sellers for a custom offer based on the same scope. Choose value, not just the lowest price.
Bundle Related Tasks: If you need multiple similar items (e.g., 3 banners), request a package price in one order.
Be Specific Upfront: Clear briefs reduce rework. Share references, examples, and acceptance criteria.
Choose the Right Tier: For simple tasks, a standard package may be enough. Use premium tiers for complex or time-sensitive work.
Plan Revisions: Keep a small buffer for one extra revision instead of adding new scope mid-project.

FAQs

Quick answers about budgeting and working with sellers.

Can I always save 50%?

No guarantee. Some projects cost less with careful scoping and comparison, while others require higher budgets for quality or speed. Results vary.

How do I avoid scope creep?

Agree on deliverables, file formats, and milestones before ordering. Any new requests should be a separate offer.

What if the delivery doesn’t match the brief?

Request revisions within the order. If issues remain, document specifics and contact support with your evidence.

Do discount codes matter?

Occasional promos can help, but the biggest savings usually come from clear scope and bundling related tasks.
